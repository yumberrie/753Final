Los Altos

It’s after the fog rolls back, over
foot hills that to me could be mountains:
my parents laugh, those? Mountains? I know
nothing of living here. A street 
with our name in the mountain-hills
but so long ago that the tree has branched
too far for us to claim any real loyalty 
or pedigree. Someway or another, we fell
far, rogue apple, rogue me, none of them
know me. My parents blinded by their hazy
nostalgia. See, those hazy skies, swallowing
smog, burnt out air; see, it all lies upon 
lies about dead hills called golden, 
just to feel like it’s special.

Capsule

Ginkgo tracks, packages of past. I know you
are special.

Imprint on stone to be buried, beneath earth and grass,
beneath the dead and the living’s tumbling steps.
You’ll miss a thousand flights to a different sky.

The falling tree cares not if we
are there to validate it. Pressure released
still trembles the forest floor.

(Will you keep going without me? There’s no proof–
no, none at all.)

The sister in white, fellow

The sister in white, fellow
white. She speaks words from paper
we’re told we need to read. Words
that stain us into an us, drag

the her and me down
to earthy centers, held tight
and entombed, to turn into
rich rots. The flower may

grow from these hearts,
but the hearts will lose
themselves, decompose. For the sister,
this is smiling. I turn

towards window, past
tree to clouds, and the words
sit like the crows. Dark shadows,
pressing and loud. Pressing and loud.

Pomme

Peel the apple, skin
that tears like paper - dead
but with memories of living.

Below is still flesh,
snapping with juice that rolls
down the chin in sweet trails.

Oh, sugar tears and salt.
You used to shine, and now
just cry. I am bitten, I am bare.
